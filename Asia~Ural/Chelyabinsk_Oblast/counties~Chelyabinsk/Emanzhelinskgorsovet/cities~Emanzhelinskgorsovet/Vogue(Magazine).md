# [[Vogue(Magazine)]] 

#is_/same_as :: [[../../../../../../../../../WikiData/WD~Vogue,154008|WD~Vogue,154008]]  

## #has_/text_of_/abstract 

> Vogue (stylized in all caps), also known as American Vogue, 
> is a monthly fashion magazine that covers style news, 
> including haute couture, fashion, beauty, culture, living, and runway. 
> 
> It is part of the global collection of Condé Nast's VOGUE media. 
> Since 2025, Chloe Malle as overseen the magazine's editorial content. 
> Anna Wintour served as editor-in-chief of the publication from 1988 to 2025 
> and now leads global operations for the publication 
> as Global Chief Content Officer and Global Editorial Director 
> overseeing Vogue and other Condé Nast titles.
>
> Headquartered at One World Trade Center 
> in the Financial District of Lower Manhattan, 
> Vogue began in 1892 as a weekly newspaper 
> before becoming a monthly magazine years later. 
> 
> Since its founding, Vogue has featured numerous 
> actors, musicians, models, athletes, and other prominent celebrities.
>
> British Vogue, launched in 1916, was the first international edition, 
> while the Italian version Vogue Italia 
> has been called the top fashion magazine in the world. 
> 
> As of March 2025, there are 28 international editions. 
> Eleven of these editions are published by Condé Nast directly: 
> - British Vogue, 
> - Vogue Arabia, 
> - Vogue Hong Kong, 
> - Vogue Deutsch, 
> - Vogue España, 
> - Vogue France, 
> - Vogue India, 
> - Vogue Italia, 
> - Vogue Japan, 
> - Vogue México y Latinoamérica, and 
> - Vogue Taiwan 
> 
> The remaining 16 editions are published as licensees of Vogue.
>
> [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vogue%20(magazine))
